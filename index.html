Laboratory of Meng Chen at UC Riverside - Laboratory Assistant. While being a laboratory assistant in Meng Chen's lab, I have obtained a lot of new techniques and knowledge on the world of laboratory science, especially in the realm of phytochrome signaling. In this lab, I perform laboratory tests including pre- and post-analytical phases such as PCR, gel electrophoresis, surface sterilazation, hypocotyl length determination, basta screening, and much more. I also collect and prepare 100+ samples/specimen daily to be test on, analyzed, and processed. For instance, by using programs to visualize data from gel electrophoresis, results can be obtained to show whether or not a plant is heterozygous or homozygous. This can then determine whether or not the plant has a specific allele that we are looking for. The goal for th elab is to elucidate signaling mechanisms by which light alters plant morphology and photosynthesis via dynamic reprogramming of nuclear and plastid genomes. My current responsibility is focused on the LRB-PHYB-PIF4 project. This project revolves around figuring out of LRB is linked and associated with phyB. Since phyB is a phytochrome, it can detect light and temperature. Essentiallu, with this detection, plants are able to grow better and taller. PhyB is known and discovered to be associated with pif4 which can detect temperature which in turn, can also stimulate better growth leading to taller plants. The goal to reach through this project is to see if irb is also associated and linked to phyB in terms of if temperature impacts plant growth as well by having irb.
